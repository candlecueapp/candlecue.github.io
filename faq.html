FAQ

Q: What is CandleCue used for?

A: CandleCue helps traders stay disciplined by tracking candle-close times on their selected timeframe—currently available for 1m, 2m, 3m, 5m, 15m, 30m, 1h, or 4h. By setting precise alerts, it reduces impulsive trades and keeps you aligned with your strategy—one candle at a time.

Q: Don’t most trading platforms already show countdowns to candle close?

A: In a way, yes—but you're still forced to watch the screen and to manually keep mental tabs. CandleCue enhances this by providing visual, audio, and vibration cues at each candle close—so you can stay focused on your trading process, whether that’s reading price action, confirming chart patterns, or analyzing footprint or Bookmap-style data.

CandleCue also allows you to customize layered alerts on top of your main timeframe. By default, you’ll get a notification at each main candle close. By adding sub-timeframe layers, you gain additional insight into market microstructure. For example, if your main timeframe is 1 hour and you add a 15-minute layer, you’ll receive 15-minute bar close alerts throughout the hour to track market rhythm across both timeframes.

Q: Can I layer more than one sub-timeframe?

A: Absolutely. For example, you can layer 5-minute, 15-minute, and 30-minute sub-timeframes into your 60-minute (1-hour) main interval.

Q: If I layer multiple sub-timeframes, what happens when several bars close at the same time?

A: CandleCue will send only the alert for the largest (i.e., highest) sub-timeframe at that moment. For instance, if your main timeframe is 1 hour and you layer both 15-minute and 30-minute alerts, you’ll receive the 30-minute bar close alert at each half-hour mark (e.g., 9:30, 10:30).

Why do we do this? Besides avoiding redundant noise:

1. Higher Timeframes = Stronger Signals. This keeps you focused on what matters most in pivotal moments.

2. Hierarchy of Decision-Making. Many traders use top-down analysis:
• Higher timeframes = trend direction
• Lower timeframes = entry/exit refinement

By emphasizing the largest closing timeframe, CandleCue keeps you aligned with the broader trend—crucial for momentum or breakout strategies.

Q: What is a custom notification? Is that the same as a layered notification?

A: Custom notifications are recurring alerts that trigger at a specific time relative to your main candle close—for example, exactly “1 minute before.” You can choose one or multiple custom alerts (e.g., 5 minutes before and 1 minute before), and they will repeat consistently with each new candle.

Layered notifications, on the other hand, are tied to additional sub-timeframe closes—such as every 5 minutes, 15 minutes, or 30 minutes—on top of your main timeframe. These help you track market structure across multiple intervals.

You can use custom and layered notifications independently or combine them. For instance, you could receive a layered alert every 15 minutes while also getting a “1 minute before” custom alert to prepare just before your main candle closes.

If a custom alert happens to coincide with a layered alert—such as a 5-minute custom alert overlapping with a 5-minute sub-timeframe layer—CandleCue will prioritize the layered alert message at that moment to avoid duplicate notifications.

Q: I'm a swing trader using the 4-hour timeframe. Can I customize when each 4-hour candle starts?

A: Yes. By default, CandleCue uses a 4-hour cycle starting from UTC 0—a common baseline for crypto traders. You can set a custom start time in Settings (e.g., 9:00 AM or 9:30 AM local time) in 30-minute increments to match your preferred trading hours or market session.

Q: Can I use CandleCue in the background?

A: Yes. Once activated, both the timer and alerts continue to function even when the app is in the background or your phone is asleep. In fact, this is one of CandleCue’s core features—helping you stay synced to market rhythms even when away from your screen. Occasionally, CandleCue may prompt you to the main page to continue receiving notifications, but the timer itself runs continouosly in the backgound at all times.

Q: When will I be prompted to return to the main page?

A: Apple enforces a maximum of 64 scheduled notifications limit per app at any one time. Depending on your selected timeframe and any layered alerts, you may reach this limit after a certain number of candles. For example, if you're using a 4-hour main timeframe with 1-hour layered alerts, you'll be prompted approximately 60 hours after the timer starts. When this happens, CandleCue will play a distinct sound and prompt you to return to the main page. By simply tapping on the prompt or reopening the app, the next batch of notifications will be scheduled instantly, and alerts will continue. The timer itself continues running in the background the entire time, and no data or alert preferences are lost—just a quick refresh to keep the system flowing.

Q: Does CandleCue require an internet connection?

A: No. CandleCue runs entirely offline using your device’s internal system clock. You only need internet access to download the app, receive updates, or access support links like the Privacy Policy or Terms of Service.

Q: Does CandleCue track my data?

A: No. CandleCue does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Your use is completely private.

Q: I don't see any ads in the app. Will CandleCue ever introduce ads?

A: No. CandleCue is designed to support focused, distraction-free trade execution and will never include ads—whether the timer is running or not. Our goal is to help you stay focused, not interrupted.
