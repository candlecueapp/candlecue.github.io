FAQ

What is CandleCue used for?

CandleCue helps traders stay disciplined by providing structured alerts to candle-close times on their selected timeframe—currently supporting 1m, 2m, 3m, 5m, 15m, 30m, 1h, and 4h. Pre-close signals and layered cues from shorter sub-timeframes provide added awareness within each candle. By setting precise, structured alerts, CandleCue is designed to reduce impulsive trades and help you stay aligned with your strategy—candle by candle.

Don’t most trading platforms already show countdowns to candle close?

Most platforms show candle countdowns—but they’re easy to miss, often displayed in small fonts that demand constant visual attention. Even more limiting, these countdowns are tied to the chart you’re actively viewing—meaning you have to keep that specific symbol open to stay on time. CandleCue enhances your charting experience by delivering clear, hands-free alerts through audio and haptic feedback—so you stay in sync without staring at the countdown or keeping one chart always in view. Optional pre-close signals and layered sub-timeframe cues further ease the cognitive load of manual tracking while enhancing market awareness and timing. Ideal for active traders who want to focus on preparation and execution—not countdowns—and swing traders who want to stay alert only when it counts.

What are custom alerts, and why do they matter?

Ever missed an entry or exit because your attention was on something else—an indicator, setup, or analysis—and the candle closed before you were ready? Custom pre-close alerts give you a head start—reminding you to refocus and prepare before the candle ends, so you’re ready to act when it counts. For example, you can set recurring reminders like “2 minutes before” to review your checklist, confirm confluence, or get into execution mode. You can even set multiple early alerts (e.g., 5 minutes and 1 minute before) to match your personal routine. These cues repeat every candle, helping you stay sharp, consistent, and timely.

What are layered alerts, and how do they differ from custom alerts?

Market structure often shifts before your main timeframe candle closes—making it easy to miss developing setups or misread early signals. Layered alerts solve this by triggering at actual sub-timeframe bar closes—like every 5 or 15 minutes within a 1-hour candle.

Instead of constantly flipping between different timeframes and reacting to unfinished candles, you get precise, confirmation-based signals across multiple timeframes. For example, if your main candle is 1 hour, layering 15-minute bars gives you actionable structure every quarter hour.

Unlike custom alerts—which notify you before a main candle closes—layered alerts provide smaller, completed timeframe cues. Together, they give you a structured, multi-layered view of market behavior—delivered with precision and without distraction.

Can I layer more than one sub-timeframe?

Absolutely. For example, you can layer 5-minute, 15-minute, and 30-minute sub-timeframes into your 60-minute (1-hour) main interval. This allows you to receive multiple bar-close notifications within each hour—offering a more detailed view of market structure across different time horizons.

If I layer multiple sub-timeframes, what happens when several timeframes close at the same time?

When multiple sub-timeframe bars close simultaneously, CandleCue will send only the notification for the largest (i.e., highest) timeframe at that moment. For example, if your main timeframe is 1 hour and you’ve layered both 15-minute and 30-minute intervals, you’ll receive the 30-minute alert at the half-hour marks (e.g., 9:30, 10:30), rather than the 15-minute one.

This helps avoid redundant signals and keeps the focus on higher timeframe closes, which often carry more weight in trade planning. It also reflects how many traders prioritize higher timeframes for trend context, while using lower ones for execution.

I'm a swing trader using the 4-hour timeframe. Can I customize when each 4-hour candle starts?

Yes. By default, CandleCue uses a 4-hour cycle starting from UTC 0—a common baseline for crypto traders. You can set a custom start time in Settings (e.g., 9:00 AM or 9:30 AM local time) in 30-minute increments to match your preferred trading hours or market session.

Can I use CandleCue in the background?

Yes. Once activated, both the timer and alerts continue to function even when the app is in the background or your phone is asleep. In fact, this is one of CandleCue’s core features—helping you stay synced to market rhythms even when away from your screen. Occasionally, CandleCue may prompt you to the main page to continue receiving notifications, but the timer itself runs continuously in the background at all times.

When will I be prompted to return to the main page?

Apple enforces a maximum of 64 scheduled notifications limit per app at any one time. Depending on your selected timeframe and any alerts, you may reach this limit after a certain number of candles. For example, if you're using a 4-hour main timeframe with 1-hour layered alerts, you'll be prompted approximately 60 hours after the timer starts. When this happens, CandleCue will play a distinct sound and prompt you to return to the main page. By simply tapping on the prompt or reopening the app, the next batch of notifications and alerts will be scheduled instantly, and you will continuously receive them. The timer itself continues running in the background the entire time, and no data or alert preferences are lost.

How is the “Current Time” determined, and how can I change it?

When you first install CandleCue, the app automatically detects your device’s local time zone and sets the UTC offset accordingly. This offset is used to display the “Current Time” at the bottom of the screen.

You can change this offset at any time in Settings under “Select UTC Offset” to view the current time in another region—such as a major financial center or preferred market.

UTC offset options range from UTC−10 to UTC+13, including fractional time zones like UTC+5:30 or UTC+9:30. This lets you align your time display with the regions or markets that matter to you.

  • UTC−10: Honolulu (Hawaiian Islands)
  • UTC−8: Los Angeles (non-DST), Vancouver (non-DST)
  • UTC−6: Chicago (non-DST), Guatemala City, Mexico City
  • UTC−5: New York (non-DST), Toronto (non-DST), Cancún, Lima, Bogotá
  • UTC+0: London (non-DST), Accra, Lisbon (non-DST)
  • UTC+1: Berlin, Paris, Rome, Madrid
  • UTC+2: Athens, Cairo, Johannesburg
  • UTC+3: Moscow, Riyadh, Istanbul, Nairobi
  • UTC+4: Dubai, Baku
  • UTC+5:30: Delhi, Mumbai, Colombo
  • UTC+7: Hanoi, Bangkok, Jakarta
  • UTC+8: Singapore, Beijing, Kuala Lumpur, Perth
  • UTC+9: Tokyo, Seoul
  • UTC+9:30: Adelaide (non-DST), Darwin
  • UTC+10: Sydney, Port Moresby
  • UTC+13: Nukuʻalofa (Tonga), Kiritimati (Kiribati)

Note: The UTC offset remains fixed until you change it manually. It does not adjust automatically for daylight saving time. This setting only affects how the current time is displayed—it does not change how alerts or timer intervals are calculated.

Does CandleCue require an internet connection?

No. CandleCue runs entirely offline using your device’s internal system clock. You only need internet access to download the app, receive updates, or access support links like the Privacy Policy or Terms of Service.

Does CandleCue track my data?

No. CandleCue does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. Your use of the app is completely private. The only data saved is your selected preferences—such as alert settings, UTC offset, or language choice—and even that is stored locally on your device. Nothing ever leaves your phone, ensuring a streamlined and secure experience.
